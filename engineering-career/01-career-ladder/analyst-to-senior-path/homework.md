# Homework — Analyst to Senior Path

## Exercise 1: Stage identification

Read the three stages (analyst → mid, mid → senior, after senior) in the README and identify which stage you are currently in. Write:

1. The specific evidence that places you in this stage (what you are doing that matches the stage description)
2. The specific evidence that you are not yet in the next stage (what you are not yet doing)
3. One stall from the "common stalls" section in the README that applies to you right now

Be honest. There are no wrong answers here — only useful ones.

## Exercise 2: Path planning

Write a one-page (or shorter) answer to: "What does the next 12 months look like if I am deliberately working toward the next level?"

Include:
- What type of work you will pursue or volunteer for (ownership, design docs, cross-functional collaboration, etc.)
- What skills or habits you will build
- What you will stop doing or do less of
- How you will make your work visible

Do not write aspirations. Write a plan specific enough that in 12 months you could assess whether you followed it.

## Exercise 3: The design doc habit

If you have never written a design doc at work, write a short one — 1-2 pages — for something you are currently working on or recently worked on. It does not have to be for a large change. It just needs to include:

1. What problem you are solving
2. What you considered doing
3. What you decided and why

Share it with a colleague for feedback, or use it as a template for the next real decision that comes along.

## Exercise 4: Opinion inventory

Think about the last design discussion or planning conversation you were in. Answer:

1. Did you have a position? Did you state it?
2. If you did not state it, why not? (Fear of being wrong? Didn't have a formed opinion? Didn't feel it was your place?)
3. What would it have looked like if you had stated a specific, reasoned position?

The goal is to identify the pattern. If the answer to "why didn't I state my opinion?" is consistently "I didn't have one," invest more in `02-engineering-opinion/forming-opinions/`. If it's consistently "I did have one but didn't say it," invest more in getting comfortable sharing opinions in lower-stakes contexts first.
